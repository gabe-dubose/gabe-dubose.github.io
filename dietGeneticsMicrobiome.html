Primary Questions:
--
How do diet and environment shape the gut microbiomes of specialized feeders?
How do gut microbiome compositon, structure, and function contribute to host phenotype?
How do host genetics and population dynamics influence their gut microbial communities?
--
Background:
--
Gut microbial communities often provide important, and sometimes necessary functions, for their host. However, it can be diffucult to infer how said microbes are acquired and evolve to occupy their host's guts. In some cases, resident gut microbes can be acquired from the host's food. In other cases, gut microbes can be transmitted from one host to another, often through fecal transmission modes. I am broadly interested in how certain microbes evolve to occupy the guts of their hosts, and how host gut microbial communities contribute to their phenotypes.
--
Cactophilic Drosophila as a model system:
--
In collaboraiton with the Haselkorn lab, I use flies that feed exclusively on cacti (cactophilic Drosophila) to study how host diet, genetics, and population structures influence their gut microbiota. Overall, we are interested in comparing and contrasting the gut microbiomes of different cactophilic Drosophila species, aiming to understand the influences of host relatedness and diet.
