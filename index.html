Overview

My name is Gabe DuBose and I'm currently a Ph.D. student in the Population Biology, Ecology, and Evolution program at Emory University. I am generally interested in how evolutionary change is facilitated and constrainted as it pertains to understanding patterns of biological diversification and complexity. My current research focuses on the evolution of life cycle complexity and the evolution of endosymbiotic interactions. I like to study these topics from both genetic and ecological perspectives.

In addition to research, I also really enjoy teaching. I have been a teaching assistant for several introductory biology courses, as well as a co-developer/co-instructor of a course on microbial ecology. I hope to continue to gain teaching experience throughout my Ph.D.
