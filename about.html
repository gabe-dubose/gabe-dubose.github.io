        Hi! I'm Gabe.

        I am currently a graduate student of bioinformatics at Georgia Tech
        who is boradly interested in "Major Transitions in Evolution."
        While working on my B.S. in Biology at the University of Central Arkansas,
        my thesis research focused on the development and stability of symbiotic
        interactions. Here, I used the social amoeba D. discoideum and several of its
        bacterial symbionts as a model system to study the context dependency of
        symbiotic interactions as it pretains to the evolutionary trajectory a symbiosis
        will follow (i.e., whether it progresses towards parasitism or mutualism).
        As a M.S. student, I will be joining the Ratcliff lab at Georgia Tech in the
        summer of 2022, where I will be studying the origins of multicellular life
        and early cell differentiation. I also hope to stay with the Ratcliff lab
        for my Ph.D. as well. More on this coming soon!

        Outside of the lab, I enjoy hiking, going to local farmers markets, and
        doing DIY condo remodels. Recently, I have gotten into webpage design
        as sort of a creative outlet. Therefore, I put my programming skills
