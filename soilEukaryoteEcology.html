Primary Questions:
--
What abiotic factors influence microbial eukaryote community composition and structure?
How do microbial eukaryotes influence prokaryotic communities?
--
Background:
--
The bacterial portion of most microbiomes usually receives the most attention. However, microbial eukaryotes can play major roles in their respective microbiomes, and represent a large diversity that has been less studied. I am broadly interested in better understanding this diversity, what factors shape eukaryotic soil microbial communities, and how microbial eukaryotes influence their local microbial communities.
--
Soil-dwelling amoeba as a model organism:
--
In collaboration with the Haselkorn lab, we have started sampling soil-dwelling social amoeba communities. Past methods for sampling social amoeba diversity have used cuture-based and morpholigical-based methods. We hope to gain more comphrensive samples through sequencing-based approaches, aiming to relate social amoeba community composition and structure to biotic and abiotic soil factors.
