       How do symbiotic relationships shift along the parasitism-mutualism spectrum?
       Symbiotic interactions range forom parasitim to mutualism, and where a symbiont falls on this
       spectrum can change depending on a variety of factors governed by the host, symbiont, and the
       environment.


       How do microbes transition to an endosymbiotic lifestyle?
       Transitioning from a free-living microbe to an endosymbiont can be a complex process where
       symbiont transmission and host-symbiont conflict greatly influences the stability and
       nature of the interaction.


       How do environmental factors influence host-symbiont interactions?
       In order for initial symbiotic interactions to presist, the host's fitness must not
       be too negatively affected. Therefore, the stress a new host receives from the
       environment can play a role in the trajectory and outcome of new symbioses.
